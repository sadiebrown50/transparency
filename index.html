Fashion Transparency Rankings Of Top Designers

As fashion weeks wrap up across the globe, advocates remind consumers of the environmental and humanitarian concerns caused by the fashion industry.

By: Sadie Brown

October 14, 2021

Protest sign at London Fashion Week Credit: Clem Onojeghuo via unspalsh

Top designers set the standard for clothing consumption across the world. But time and again, data shows that the fashion industry is marred by waste, pollution and human rights abuses. In fact, a climate activist stormed the Louis Vuitton catwalk in Paris last week with a banner reading, “overconsumption = extinction”.

In 2016, Fashion Revolution started the Fashion Transparency Index, a yearly review of the top grossing fashion brands which grades them on transparency in a variety of different areas like: wages, sustainability and - this year - their response to the COVID-19 pandemic. Below is how just a few of the big names on the Fashion Week runway rank in the 2021 Fashion Transparency Index.

It's important to note that the Fashion Transparency Index ranks transparancey in public disclosures, not specific impact of policies.

Policy

Each brand is scored based on public disclosures about policies for labor, human rights, sustainability etc.

Louis Vuitton: 80.3%

Dior: 80.3%

Tom Ford: 1.5%

Gucci: 88.6%

Traceability

Each brand is scored based on public disclosures about its supply chain.

Louis Vuitton: 1.3%

Dior: 1.3%

Tom Ford: 0%

Gucci: 31%

COVID-19

This year, brands were also scored based on the transparency of their response to the COVID-19 pandemic.

Louis Vuitton: 0%

Dior: 0%

Tom Ford: 0%

Gucci: 60%
